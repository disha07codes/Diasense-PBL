Diabetes Awareness

Knowledge is the first step towards prevention and control

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body processes blood sugar (glucose). Glucose is the main source of energy for your body, and insulin helps move glucose from the bloodstream into the cells.

Types of Diabetes

  • Type 1: The body does not produce insulin.
  • Type 2: The body does not use insulin properly.
  • Gestational Diabetes: Occurs during pregnancy.

Why Monitoring Blood Sugar Matters

Regular monitoring helps prevent serious complications such as heart disease, kidney damage, vision loss, and nerve problems. Early awareness allows better lifestyle management.

Myths & Facts

  • Myth: Eating sugar causes diabetes.
  • Fact: Lifestyle, genetics, and insulin resistance play major roles.
  • Myth: Diabetes is not serious.
  • Fact: Uncontrolled diabetes can lead to severe health issues.

Prevention & Healthy Lifestyle

  • Maintain a balanced diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Manage stress
  • Get enough sleep
  • Monitor blood glucose levels
